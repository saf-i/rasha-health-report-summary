At a glance

What matters most

A practical scan of what to confirm, what to treat together, and what can be monitored routinely.

Confirm first

Possible active sacroiliitis

Rheumatology review is the highest-value next step because it may change long-term treatment.

Rehab focus

Hip, knee, SI joint, and lumbar mechanics

One integrated PT plan is more useful than treating each painful area separately.

Finish tests

Derm labs, lymph node follow-up, hearing tests

These are not the most urgent issues, but they prevent guessing and missed correctable causes.

Monitor

Skin rash, ENT/allergy, incidental pelvic/coccyx findings

Usually routine unless symptoms worsen or new warning signs appear.

Start sequence

Suggested order of operations

01

Rheumatology

Evaluate suspected active/chronic sacroiliitis and inflammatory arthritis pattern.

02

Integrated PT

Treat SI, lumbar, hip, and knee mechanics as one movement system.

03

Orthopedics

Follow hip FAI/labrum and knee effusion/Baker cyst if symptoms persist.

04

Derm labs

Complete hair-loss labs and lymph node ultrasound if still planned.

05

ENT testing

Finish hearing and Eustachian tube tests; continue allergy treatment as directed.
