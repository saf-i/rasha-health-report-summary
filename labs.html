Baseline safety / organ function

Useful before treatment decisions

Iron detail

Useful if ferritin is low or unclear

Serum ironTIBCTransferrin saturation

Ferritin can be affected by inflammation, so a fuller iron panel may clarify true iron availability.

Hormone / androgen workup

Discuss if chin hair growth, acne, irregular cycles, or PCOS concern exists

Total/free testosteroneDHEA-SLH/FSHProlactinA1c or fasting insulin if PCOS/metabolic risk is suspected

Helps determine whether androgen excess or PCOS-like physiology is contributing to hair or skin findings.

Infection screening before advanced rheumatology meds

Only if treatment path requires it

TB screeningHepatitis B/COther tests chosen by rheumatology

Often required before immunosuppressive therapy, but not usually the first test unless that treatment is being considered.

What would make the reports easier to understand?

The most useful missing information is whether inflammation markers and rheumatology markers support a single inflammatory condition connecting the SI joints, hip, knee, and back. The second most useful information is whether thyroid, iron, vitamin, or hormone abnormalities explain the hair findings.
